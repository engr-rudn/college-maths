# Equicontinuity
 A family of functions $$
 \mathcal{F}(X)
 $$ in the space of continuous functions 
 $$C(X)$$ is *equicontinuous* if and only if, 
 > for all $$\epsilon>0$$, there exists a 
 > $$
 \delta >0
 $$ 
 such that 
 > $$
 \forall f \in \mathcal{F}
 $$ and 
 > $$
 \forall a,b \in X, (|x-y| < \delta \implies |f(x)-f(y)| < \epsilon)
 $$.
